Pharmacology Weak Area
A nurse is administering heparin to a patient with deep vein thrombosis. The patient's aPTT result is reported as 120 seconds (normal therapeutic range: 60–100 seconds). Which of the following is the nurse's priority action?
Correct Answer: B — Rationale
An aPTT of 120 seconds is supratherapeutic (therapeutic target is 60–100 seconds). This indicates excessive anticoagulation and places the patient at serious risk of bleeding. The nurse must hold the infusion and notify the physician immediately. Protamine sulfate (Option C) is the antidote but requires a physician's order — the nurse cannot administer it independently without a prescription.

💊 Pharmacology · Tap to reveal
What is the mechanism of action of heparin, and what monitoring parameter is used to assess its therapeutic effectiveness?
Tap card to flip
✅ Answer
aPTT Monitoring
Heparin acts by activating antithrombin III, which inhibits thrombin (Factor IIa) and Factor Xa, preventing the conversion of fibrinogen to fibrin. Therapeutic effectiveness is monitored by the activated partial thromboplastin time (aPTT), maintained at 1.5–2.5× the control value (approximately 60–100 seconds).
